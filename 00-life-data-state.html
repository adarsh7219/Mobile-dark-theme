The Comoros Islands are the perfect destination to get away as it is removed from normal reality having beautiful beaches, rainforests, and mountains to hike through. It is one of the world's poorest countries. Economic growth and poverty reduction are major priorities for the government. The climate is generally tropical and mild. The islands are rarely subject to cyclones. There is a high rate of unemployment and the majority of the working sector is employed in the primary sector. This economy is involved in agriculture, fishing, hunting, and forestry. Most of the population of Comoros consists of the non-working age group. More than half of the population is literate and the remaining are hampered from such opportunities due to lack of teachers and poor results.

Albania

[ Know more ]

Albania, a formerly closed, centrally-planned state, is a developing country with a modern open-market economy. The resourcefulness of the people has seen the country pick itself up after the downfall of communism and is beginning to thrive. Considered for generations the most insular, mysterious, and secretive nation in Europe, Albania has broken away from its troubled past. Albanians are resilient, patriotic, welcoming, and friendly. The influence of the Ottoman Empire, under which Albania was subject from the Middle Ages until the early 20th century, is apparent in the large, Turkish-style architectural feats. It is one of the poorest countries in Europe. Albania is one of the countries in Europe most at risk from natural disasters according to the UN.
